Methodology

Our hypothesis claims riders below 40 years of age tend to have longer trips as compared to riders of 40 or older.
The null hypothesis would be the average trip duration for riders equal and above the age of 40 is greater than or equal to the average trip duration for riders less than the age of 40. The alternative hypothesis would be the average trip duration for riders equal and above the age of 40 is less than the average trip duration for riders less than the age of 40.
i.e.,
\(H_0:\ \ \mu_A\ \ge\ \mu_B\)
\(H_1:\ \ \mu_A\ <\ \mu_B\)
where  \(\mu_A\) is the average trip duration for riders equal and above the age of 40 
\(\mu_B\) is the average trip duration for riders less than the age of 40.
We received two peer reviews, suggesting the z-test and t-test for our testing. We are testing whether the mean of one sample is greater than the mean of the other sample. For this purpose, the one tailed t-test seems the more appropriate choice, and we went ahead with it.
